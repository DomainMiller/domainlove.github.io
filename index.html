Autonomous Path Planning for Space Robots Based on Vision Measurement

The research background for this project is the placement and trajectory planning problem of a free-floating space robot, and a space robot system based on visual measurement has been designed. Initially, monocular vision is used to preliminarily measure the cooperative target, and the target's motion is estimated based on the measurement results. According to the predicted target motion velocity, velocity planning is carried out for both the end of the robot arm and the base. With the determined end-effector velocity, velocity-level inverse kinematics is employed to solve for the joint angular velocities, thus controlling joint movements. Finally, forward kinematics recalculates the position and orientation of the robot arm's end. Additionally, a system model was built in SIMULINK, incorporating a PID control module to adjust relevant parameters until the joint movements were smooth, and visualized the process of the space robot placing the target.

This undergraduate capstone project focused on developing and simulating control strategies for a space robot designed to capture non-cooperative targets. Key achievements and methodologies include:

  • System Modeling: Established a three-degree-of-freedom space robot system using Lagrangian equations, multibody kinematics, and dynamics to accurately represent the robot‚Äôs behavior.
  • Dual-Loop Proportional-Integral-Derivative Control: Designed a collaborative control system for the robot‚Äôs base platform and manipulator, effectively addressing nonlinear coupling and ensuring precise motion planning and trajectory execution.
  • Deep Reinforcement Learning Integration: Implemented Soft Q-Learning algorithms to train adaptive target capture strategies, enhancing the robot‚Äôs ability to handle dynamic conditions and improve task execution through intelligent decision-making.
  • Collision Force Consideration: Incorporated collision force dynamics to refine capture precision, ensuring robust performance under various disturbances and nonlinear interactions.

Dynamic Topology and Fault-Tolerant Control in Heterogeneous Formations

This project explores a comprehensive approach to controlling a formation of heterogeneous satellites under communication delays, dynamic topology changes, and various fault modes. By integrating advanced theoretical concepts into an existing simulation framework, the goal is to ensure stable and efficient cooperation among satellites despite differences in their dynamics, external disturbances, and actuator faults.

  • Heterogeneous Dynamics: Each agent may have distinct system matrices, control inputs, and nonlinearities, allowing the method to handle structural and functional diversity within the formation.
  • Dynamic Topology Switching: Using a semi-Markov chain model, the network topology can switch among multiple predefined configurations, representing communication link changes or structural faults in real time.
  • Improved Fault Representation and Handling: The proposed scheme accommodates both multiplicative (efficiency decline) and additive (external disturbance) faults. A unified fault model enables comprehensive detection and rapid compensation.
  • Game-Theoretic and Approximate Optimal Control: By framing faults as adversarial players in a differential game, and employing a generalized fuzzy hyperbolic model (GFHM) to approximate value functions, the method reduces complexity and computational overhead. This approach ensures that, under various fault scenarios, the satellites maintain stable tracking behavior, achieve convergence, and effectively adapt their control gains. The approximate value functions and adaptive weight updates confirm the robustness and improved performance over traditional, more conservative methods.

Hybrid Control Modeling and Simulation for Heavy Truck Platooning

This project focuses on the modeling and simulation of a heavy truck platoon formation scenario using hybrid systems theory. The approach captures both continuous dynamics (vehicle motion and spacing) and discrete events (formation changes), ensuring that multiple vehicles operating under uncertain conditions can merge into a single stable platoon.

By representing each vehicle as an agent with position and velocity states, the system integrates continuous evolution with sudden jumps triggered when certain conditions are met‚Äîsuch as two vehicles aligning their positions. To manage these transitions and maintain stability, logical variables are introduced to record platoon membership and prevent unnecessary repeated jumps once the formation is established. The result is a hybrid control framework that guarantees the eventual convergence of all vehicles to a common velocity and position set, forming a unified platoon in finite time.

  • Hybrid System Framework: Unified continuous position updates with discrete jump events to model complex platoon dynamics.
  • Logic Variables for Memory: Ensured that once vehicles merge, the system avoids infinite jump loops and stabilizes the platoon configuration.
  • Robust Convergence Guarantees: Demonstrated finite-time convergence to a stable platoon, enabling reliable operation despite initial separation and varying conditions.

Multi-agent Control under Markovian DoS Attacks

This project explores a multi-vehicle coordination control strategy under a Markovian switching sampling framework with intermittent Denial-of-Service (DoS) attacks. By modeling the leader-follower platooning system in discrete time and incorporating time-varying sampling intervals, we leverage state feedback control gains to achieve consensus and maintain stable formation despite disturbances. The approach involves simulating three follower vehicles tracking a leader‚Äôs trajectory while adapting control inputs based on a Markov chain governing the sampling rates. Results demonstrate that, even under random DoS attacks and nonlinear perturbations (e.g., sinusoidal disturbances), the proposed strategy ensures robust and reliable convergence of follower vehicles to desired positions and velocities.

Lying on the Couch

My second book of Points of Views (POV), truly astonishing! The author uses the POV technique to create a wonderful suspense story and also provides a wealth of psychological knowledge. For instance, medications for treating depression such as Risperidone and Paxil are discussed. The writing is deeply pragmatic, featuring a sensitive and impressionable new therapist as the male protagonist, an ambitious and ruthless mentor, and a strong yet vulnerable female lead. The story begins with a veteran consultant embroiled in a sexual scandal with a patient. He argues that engaging genuinely with patients rather than maintaining a professional distance can be more beneficial. It seems his patient has indeed changed significantly, but there might be more to the story. Is this the truth, or just an excuse he concocted to seduce his patient?
